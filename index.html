About Me

With extensive experience in geospatial analysis, I blend technical expertise and strategic thinking to help clients navigate complex, location-based challenges. My work is focused on two core areas:

  • GIS in Politics: I apply spatial analysis to political campaigns, initiatives, and elections using R. I’m also experienced with ArcGIS Pro, ArcGIS Online, QGIS, and Carto, which allows me to tailor solutions to meet diverse project needs.
  • Applied Spatial Analysis & Mapping: I build clear, decision-ready spatial products—interactive web maps, dashboards, and reproducible workflows—turning complex datasets into actionable insights.

Boston Crime Hot Spot Analysis (Patrol Shift Planning)

This project identifies statistically significant clusters of reported incidents across Boston and translates them into actionable patrol-focus areas. The workflow produces hotspot maps and exports that can support shift planning, resource allocation, and targeted outreach. Built in R with spatial statistics and reproducible mapping outputs.

Portland, Maine Flood Risk

This project maps flood risk exposure in the Portland, Maine area and translates it into decision-ready outputs for planning and mitigation. Where flooding is most likely is only half the story—what matters is who and what is in the path. The workflow pairs flood-risk surfaces with population and context layers to highlight where impacts are most likely and where interventions can be prioritized. Built in R with reproducible geospatial analysis and web-ready map exports designed for briefings and stakeholder discussions; when appropriate data are available, the same approach can be refined to the parcel level for property-specific insight.
